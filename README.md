Africa Digital Readiness Analysis (2000–2024)

Project Overview

This project analyzes digital readiness across African countries between 2000 and 2024 using the World Bank’s World Development Indicators (WDI) dataset. The objective is to assess Africa’s progress in digital transformation by examining key indicators related to digital access, infrastructure, economic capacity, and human development.

The project was developed as the final capstone project for the AnalystLab Africa Data Analytics Internship and demonstrates the complete data analytics workflow, from data preparation to visualization and insight generation using Power BI.

⸻

Problem Statement

Digital technologies continue to shape economic growth, innovation, and social development. However, digital progress across Africa remains uneven.

This project seeks to answer the following question:

How has Africa’s digital readiness evolved between 2000 and 2024?

By analyzing multiple development indicators, the project identifies long-term trends, country-level differences, and the relationship between digital readiness and economic development.

⸻

Objectives

The objectives of this project are to:

* Assess the level of digital readiness across African countries.
* Examine digital transformation trends between 2000 and 2024.
* Compare digital readiness across countries.
* Explore the relationship between digital readiness and economic development.
* Provide actionable insights and recommendations for policymakers and development stakeholders.

⸻

Dataset

Source: World Bank – World Development Indicators (WDI)

The World Development Indicators (WDI) is the World Bank’s flagship database of global development statistics. It provides comprehensive data across sectors including technology, infrastructure, economy, education, health, and governance.

Indicators Used

The Digital Readiness Index was developed using six key indicators:

* Internet Users (% of Population)
* Mobile Cellular Subscriptions
* Fixed Broadband Subscriptions
* Access to Electricity
* GDP per Capita
* Life Expectancy at Birth

These indicators were selected because they collectively represent the key dimensions that enable digital transformation.

Data Preparation

The dataset underwent several preprocessing steps before analysis:

* Filtered the dataset to include African countries only.
* Selected indicators relevant to digital readiness.
* Removed unnecessary fields.
* Addressed missing values where applicable.
* Standardized data formats.
* Created calculated measures using DAX.
* Developed a composite Digital Readiness Index for analysis.

⸻

Dashboard Features

The interactive dashboard includes:

* KPI Cards
* Line Chart showing digital readiness trends
* Scatter Plot illustrating the relationship between digital readiness and GDP per capita
* Map showing the spatial distribution of development across Africa
* Column Chart ranking the leading African countries
* Interactive filters and slicers

⸻

Key Findings

* Africa experienced a gradual improvement in digital readiness between 2000 and 2024.
* Countries with higher digital readiness generally recorded higher GDP per capita.
* Digital development remains uneven across the continent, revealing a significant digital divide.
* A small number of countries consistently outperform others in digital readiness.
* Improvements in electricity access and internet connectivity contribute significantly to overall digital development.

⸻

Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following recommendations are proposed:

* Increase investments in digital infrastructure, particularly broadband connectivity.
* Expand access to reliable electricity to support digital adoption.
* Promote affordable internet access across underserved communities.
* Strengthen policies that encourage digital innovation and inclusion.
* Foster regional collaboration to reduce disparities in digital development.
